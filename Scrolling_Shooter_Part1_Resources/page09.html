Summary

Your game is now finished! Why not test it now and see how it plays? As you will no doubt find, it's simple and not terribly polished, but it's a game, it's fun for a short while, and you made it... So you should be pleased with yourself! You should also now know a number of important things about GameMaker: Studio and making games:
  • Variables are used for many things, like storing values or controlling actions.

  • Variables can have a global scope or an instance scope.

  • There are a number of built-in variables to make programming your games easier.

  • Small rooms can be made to look "infinite" with scrolling backgrounds.

  • You can use Alarms to control the timing of things.

  • In collision events, actions marked as "other" will affect the other instance in the collision
That's quite a lot for such a simple game, but it's still not quite enough. The game has no score, and your player plane being destroyed on a single hit is rather frustrating. The randomness of the enemy planes is also something that quickly becomes boring. However these are all things that can be fixed, and in Part 2 of this tutorial we will cover them as well as the use of views and a few other things to turn this into a real game so when you are ready, download the second part and see just how we can turn a mediocre game into a great game!
